Founder

Julieta Reyes

Organizational & Business Development

Nineteen years at the intersection of diplomacy, defense, communication, culture, and strategy. Fluent in four languages. Field experience across over thirty-five countries on six continents. Former Chair of Diversity & Inclusion at the Embassy of the Kingdom of the Netherlands.

Her formation began not in a classroom but at altitude. Under the mentorship of Evelyn Wong, Flight Safety Instructor and Chief Cabin Crew, a leader who made discipline and preparedness as essential as a first aid kit; Julieta learned presence and the art of performing at the highest standard regardless of circumstance.

In a concentrated passage of five to eight years, she traveled across over thirty-five countries and six continents. From Johannesburg and Pretoria to São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro and Brasília. From Singapore, Bangkok and Kuala Lumpur to Sydney. From Athens, Rome and Paris to Tel Aviv. From Quito and Bogotá to Caracas, San José and Mexico City. Across the Caribbean islands she knows as home; and through more than twenty-six states of the United States, Puerto Rico and the US Virgin Islands, and Toronto, Canada.

Those journeys brought her face to face with the full spectrum of how societies are organized: their brilliance and their failures, their customs and their contradictions. Human rights, political systems, child labor, human trafficking, capital punishment, economic disparity — witnessed firsthand, not studied at a distance. It is from that vantage point that her commitment to inclusive, ethical and humane leadership was forged. Not as a philosophy. As a conviction. Leadership that performs, and leaves no one behind.

At the Consulate and Embassy of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, she worked alongside three Consul Generals, five Defense Attachés, and in the orbit of eight Ambassadors, three Chiefs of Defense, and three Ministers of Defense. A position originally designed for military Aides-de-Camp had been reimagined as a civilian role. Julieta stepped into it as a woman and a minority, in an environment shaped by military culture, diplomatic protocol, and institutional precision. Within six months she had mastered the environment and grown into departmental leadership.

Her years at the Embassy placed her in close proximity to some of the most consequential military leadership of her generation. Figures such as General Tom Middendorp and Admiral Rob Bauer led not through directives alone but through presence, policy, and a clarity of vision that left a visible mark on the organizations they commanded. Julieta observed this carefully — and that understanding now informs everything she brings to her clients.
