About

A Life Spent
at Every Table.

Born in Curaçao to Mexican parents, Julieta Reyes grew up between islands, languages, and worlds. Her father, a professional drummer who traveled the globe, gave her something no curriculum could: the conviction that the world is worth understanding from the inside. That conviction has guided every chapter of her career since.

Her formation began not in a classroom but at altitude. Under the mentorship of Evelyn Wong, Flight Safety Instructor and Chief Cabin Crew, a leader who made discipline and preparedness as essential as a first aid kit; Julieta learned presence and the art of performing at the highest standard regardless of circumstance. The world became her education. She observed it the way an anthropologist would: carefully, without assumptions.

In a concentrated passage of five to eight years, she traveled across over thirty-five countries and six continents. From Johannesburg and Pretoria to São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro and Brasília. From Singapore, Bangkok and Kuala Lumpur to Sydney. From Athens, Rome and Paris to Tel Aviv. From Quito and Bogotá to Caracas, San José and Mexico City. Across the Caribbean islands she knows as home; and through more than twenty-six states of the United States, Puerto Rico and the US Virgin Islands, and Toronto, Canada.

Those journeys shaped far more than cultural fluency. They brought her face to face with the full spectrum of how societies are organized: their brilliance and their failures, their customs and their contradictions. Human rights, political systems, child labor, human trafficking, capital punishment, economic disparity — witnessed firsthand, not studied at a distance. It is from that vantage point that her commitment to inclusive, ethical and humane leadership was forged. Not as a philosophy. As a conviction. Leadership that performs, and leaves no one behind.

What followed was nineteen years at the intersection of diplomacy, defense, communication, culture, and organizational strategy across Curaçao, Aruba, the United States, the Netherlands, and Mexico. At the Consulate and Embassy of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, she worked alongside three Consul Generals, five Defense Attachés, and in the orbit of eight Ambassadors, three Chiefs of Defense, and three Ministers of Defense. She did not simply observe this world. She earned its trust.

A position originally designed for military Aides-de-Camp had been reimagined as a civilian role. Julieta stepped into it as a woman and a minority, in an environment shaped by military culture, diplomatic protocol, and institutional precision. Within six months she had mastered the environment and grown into departmental leadership. It remains one of the defining chapters of her career, and the source of the standard she brings to every engagement.

Her work produced tangible outcomes: landmark cultural and public diplomacy initiatives, a DEIB framework that became institutional policy, and a record of delivery that earned lasting institutional trust.

Her years at the Embassy also placed her in close proximity to some of the most consequential military leadership of her generation. Figures such as General Tom Middendorp and Admiral Rob Bauer led not through directives alone but through presence, policy, and a clarity of vision that left a visible mark on the organizations they commanded. Julieta observed this carefully. She understood what distinguished leaders who merely occupied a role from those who transformed it. That understanding now informs everything she brings to her clients.

Fluent in four languages. Rooted in four countries. Trusted by diplomats, military institutions, and executives alike. Julieta now brings that rare convergence of cultural intelligence, institutional discipline, and strategic clarity to organizations ready to lead with intention.

The Reyes Group is the next chapter. Yours may be too.
